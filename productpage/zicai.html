ZHICAI PIA (RM 75)

Ingredients : Flour, sticky rice noodles, eggs, coconut milk, granulated sugar

Expired Date : 2 week

Weight : 500 gram

Calories : 1500 kcal

History : ... Honeycomb cake or bee sinus is a pastry that spreads around the Malay Peninsula. It is mixed with flour, sticky rice flour, eggs, coconut milk, and sugar to make a batter. Let the batter be dipped on the cake mold installed in the honeycomb, and then put into the heat Deep-fried in oil, the finished product resembles a large honeycomb or a geometric pattern of large round petals, which tastes thin and crisp. Although the material is simple and cheap, the production process is cumbersome. It takes a long time to stand by the hot oil, shake the cake mold until the batter comes off, and then use chopsticks to constantly flip each hole of the pastry to let it be shaped in the hot oil. In the hot climate of Malaysia, the making process is very difficult, so this pastry is usually only seen in Chinese New Year, Malay Eid or Indian Deepavali. Its appearance is similar to that of the "Sugar Ring" circulated in southern China, but the production method is different.
